What LARAS means

Balance, harmony, and being in tune.

Laras is the Indonesian word for being aligned, grounded, and in tune with yourself — not just resting, but truly returning.

Start with intention

Not just a candle. A ritual of return.

Four signatures, each built around a different emotional need. Take the assessment to find yours — or explore all four.

Why this brand exists

Most self-care products offer comfort, but not connection.

Urban professional women are constantly managing pressure, overstimulation, and emotional fatigue — yet most self-care products only offer surface-level comfort instead of helping them feel grounded and reconnected.

  • Self-care is often passive, aesthetic, and generic.
  • Different people carry different stories, needs, and struggles.
  • Fragrance becomes more meaningful when it feels guided and emotionally true.
LARAS transforms self-care into an intentional ritual through premium Indonesian products that combine locally rooted ingredients, emotional scent storytelling, and thoughtful design.

The long-term vision is bigger than candles: helping people reconnect with themselves, find balance, and choose rituals that feel personal to their own story.

The Ritual

LARAS isn't a product you buy once. It's a practice you return to — guided by where you actually are, not where you think you should be.

01

02

03

Begin your ritual

Each candle arrives with a ritual guide — a simple, intentional practice for lighting it. Not a routine, but a return. A moment that belongs only to you.

What's inside

Rooted in Indonesian botanicals.

Every LARAS ingredient is chosen for its emotional resonance as much as its scent — many sourced from specific islands and regions of Indonesia, where they've been used in traditional rituals for centuries.

🌸
Java, Indonesia
Melati Jawa
Jasminum sambac

The Javanese jasmine has been used in ceremonial offerings and bridal rituals for centuries. Soft, clean, and quietly sacred — it carries the weight of intention.

Found in Jeda
🌿
Aceh, Sumatra
Nilam
Pogostemon cablin

Indonesian patchouli from Aceh is considered some of the world's finest. Deep, earthy, and grounding — it anchors the mind and slows the breath. A scent that asks you to arrive.

Found in Temu Diri
🌾
Nationwide, Indonesia
Pandan Wangi
Pandanus amaryllifolius

Pandan is the scent of home in Indonesian cooking — warm, slightly sweet, unmistakably familiar. In Pulang, it carries the emotional memory of being somewhere safe and loved.

Found in Pulang
🌱
Java & Bali
Serai Wangi
Cymbopogon nardus

Lemongrass from Java has a clean, bright quality that feels like movement — like the first real breath after a long time indoors. Uplifting without being loud, it's the opening note of renewal.

Found in Rintik
